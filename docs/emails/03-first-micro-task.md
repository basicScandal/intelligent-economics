## Body

It's been a week since you joined Intelligent Economics. You've seen the framework. You've played with the simulator. Now it's time to contribute something real.

Below are six micro-tasks, one for each volunteer role. **Pick the one that matches your interest and do it this week.** Each takes 15 minutes or less.

---

**Economists and Theorists**

Read this: GDP was designed in 1937 to measure wartime production capacity. It was never meant to measure prosperity, well-being, or sustainability. Yet it remains the dominant metric 89 years later. Post your reaction in #economists on Discord -- what's the single biggest thing GDP gets wrong?

**Engineers and Builders**

Review our open GitHub issues tagged "good-first-issue" and comment on one you'd tackle. Even a "here's how I'd approach this" comment helps us prioritize. Find them at: https://github.com/intelligenteconomics

**Designers and Communicators**

The MIND Dashboard currently shows four progress bars. Sketch one alternative visualization -- a radar chart, a Venn diagram, an infographic, anything -- and share it in #designers on Discord. Low fidelity is fine. Ideas matter more than polish.

**Community Organizers**

Identify one local organization in your area -- a civic group, a local government innovation lab, a university research center -- that might be interested in alternative economic indicators. Post the name and a one-sentence description in #organizers on Discord.

**Researchers and Academics**

Find one academic paper published in the last 5 years on alternative economic indicators (Beyond GDP, GPI, HDI, Inclusive Wealth Index, etc.) and share the link with a 2-sentence summary in #researchers on Discord.

**Policymakers and Advocates**

Draft a 3-sentence elevator pitch for MIND aimed at a city council member. Post it in #policymakers on Discord. Bonus: if it's good enough, actually send it.
