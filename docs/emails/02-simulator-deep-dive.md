Three days ago, you joined Intelligent Economics. Today, we're giving you a tool to break things.

**Zone Zero is our interactive simulator.** It lets you manipulate the four MIND dimensions -- Material, Intelligence, Network, and Diversity -- and watch what happens to a society's prosperity in real time.

**How it works:**

Each dimension has a slider from 0 to 100. Drag them around and the visualization responds instantly. The key insight: if any single dimension drops to zero, total prosperity collapses. That's the "Zone Zero" effect -- the mathematical reality that you can't compensate for a total failure in one dimension by excelling in others.

**The four dimensions you control:**

- **Material** -- What happens when natural resources are depleted but everything else is strong?
- **Intelligence** -- What if a country defunds education and research? How fast does prosperity erode?
- **Network** -- Pull this to zero and watch what happens when social trust disappears.
- **Diversity** -- Monocultures look efficient until they're not. See why diversity is load-bearing.

**Try this experiment:** Set Material to 80, Intelligence to 90, Network to 85, and Diversity to 5. Watch what happens. Then slowly drag Diversity up and see the inflection point. That gap between 5 and 30 is where most real-world policy failures live.
