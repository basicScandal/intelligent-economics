## 1. Academic Landscape: GDP Alternatives & the MIND Model

### The Beyond GDP Movement — Current State

The field is at a critical inflection point:
- **UN High-Level Expert Group on Beyond GDP** launched May 2025, interim report November 2025 proposing ~30 indicators
- **Pact for the Future (2024)** committed nations to complementary indicators
- **EU Beyond GDP** legislative framework in progress since 2023
- **Wellbeing Economy Governments (WEGo):** Scotland, Iceland, Finland, Wales, New Zealand (NZ's 2019 Wellbeing Budget rolled back in 2024 — cautionary)

### Key Existing Frameworks

| Framework | Type | Dimensions | Aggregation | Zero-Floor? |
|-----------|------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| HDI (UNDP) | Index | 3 (health, education, income) | Geometric mean (since 2010) | No — substitutes floor values |
| GPI | Adjusted GDP | ~26 adjustments | Additive | No |
| Social Progress Index | Dashboard | 12 components, 3 dimensions | Weighted average | No |
| Doughnut Economics | Visual framework | 12 social + 9 planetary | Dashboard (no single score) | N/A |
| OECD Better Life | Interactive tool | 11 dimensions | User-weighted, additive | No |
| MIND | Multiplicative index | 4 (M×I×N×D) | Pure multiplication | **Yes — this is novel** |

### MIND's Genuine Novelty

**High Confidence (5+ sources):**
- The HDI moved to geometric mean in 2010 specifically because additive models masked dimensional weakness — this validates MIND's premise
- HDI actively engineers *around* zero-collapse by substituting floor values — MIND embraces it as a normative feature
- No existing mainstream framework treats zero in any dimension as categorically fatal
- No framework has a dedicated "Intelligence" or "non-metabolic capital" dimension

**Key Academic Targets:**
- **Sabina Alkire (OPHI, Oxford)** — multidimensional poverty, threshold methods — closest methodological neighbor
- **Stiglitz-Sen-Fitoussi Commission** — original 2009 mandate for beyond-GDP measurement
- **Key Journals:** Social Indicators Research (Springer), Journal of Economic Surveys, Ecological Economics
- **Rijpma (2025, J. of Econ Surveys)** — comprehensive historical review of composite indicators

### The Intelligence Dimension Gap

- AI capex contributed 1.1% to US GDP growth in H1 2025, exceeding dot-com era IT investment (St. Louis Fed, Jan 2026)
- Intangible capital stock understated by $3.6T in US alone (Corrado, Hulten, Sichel / NBER)
- Current frameworks treat knowledge/technology as inputs, not as first-class capital
- **No beyond-GDP framework captures cognitive/AI capital as a dimension** — MIND fills a real gap

### Strategic Positioning Recommendation

Frame MIND as **completing the HDI's unfinished 2010 reform** by:
1. Making the zero-floor property explicit and theoretically grounded (not a numerical workaround)
2. Adding the Intelligence dimension as the first framework response to AI's measurable macro impact
3. Engaging OPHI directly — they are the most likely to validate or critique

## 3. Nucleation Zone Strategies

### What Makes Special Economic Zones Succeed

**High Confidence (multiple case studies):**
1. **Geographic leverage** — proximity to existing trade routes, markets, or talent pools
2. **One-stop administration** — single regulatory window, not bureaucratic maze
3. **Sector focus** — zones that try to be "everything" fail; specificity wins
4. **Credible sovereign commitment** — legal stability that survives political cycles
5. **Infrastructure first** — physical and digital infrastructure before firms arrive

### Case Study Lessons

| Zone | Outcome | Key Lesson |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Shenzhen** | $500B+ economy from 30K village | Phased approach: light manufacturing → tech hub → innovation |
| **Singapore** | First World in one generation | Institutional quality + rule of law + human capital investment |
| **Mauritius** | Middle-income from sugar dependency | Diversification + trade openness + education |
| **Prospera (Honduras)** | $11B arbitration, sovereign conflict | **Cautionary:** sovereignty bypass creates existential legal risk |
| **Sidewalk Labs (Toronto)** | Abandoned | **Cautionary:** community trust deficit → political collapse |
| **Barcelona Superblocks** | Successful, expanding | Bottom-up, neighborhood-scale, visible quality-of-life gains |
| **Amsterdam Doughnut** | Active, data-driven | DEAL framework → city policy → measurable indicators |

### Recommended Path for Symbiotic Zones

**Use existing legal vehicles, not novel legal status:**
- Regulatory sandboxes (OECD toolkit published June 2025)
- Enterprise/opportunity zones with additional metrics
- City-level partnerships (Amsterdam Doughnut model)
- University-anchored innovation districts

**Failure modes to avoid:**
- Sovereignty bypass (Prospera)
- Community trust deficit (Sidewalk Labs)
- Political cycle vulnerability (NZ Wellbeing Budget)
- Over-ambition in scope (failed SEZs in Tanzania, Bangladesh)

## 4. Volunteer Conversion & Community Building

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Metric | Industry Baseline | Strong Performance |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|
| Visitor → email signup | 2-5% | 5-10% |
| Signup → active volunteer | 15-20% | 30%+ |
| 30-day volunteer retention | ~65% | 80%+ with structured onboarding |

### The Proven Conversion Flow

1. **Capture email first** — one field, minimal friction (NOT the full form currently on the site)
2. **Welcome within 24 hours** — single highest-leverage retention action
3. **Micro-task within 7 days** — low-commitment, visible impact
4. **Role selection after engagement** — not before (current site asks too much upfront)
5. **Buddy/point-of-contact by day 7** — personal connection drives retention
6. **Structured 30-day touchpoint plan** — the make-or-break window

### Movement Models That Worked

- **Obama/MyBO (2008):** 2.2M volunteers, $200M+ raised. Key: gave people infrastructure to lead, not just follow
- **350.org:** Decentralized, local group autonomy with strategic frame. 22%+ peer-to-peer reply rates
- **Extinction Rebellion:** Self-replication as growth mechanism. Shared identity + training materials
- **EA:** Explicit funnel model (awareness → engaged → core). Non-linear paths matter

### Platform Recommendations

| Stage | Tool | Why |
|-------|------|-----|
| **MVP (now)** | Formspree + Buttondown newsletter | Free, 30 minutes to set up |
| **Growing (100+ volunteers)** | NationBuilder or Mobilize | Unified CMS + database + comms |
| **Scale (1000+)** | Custom + Solidarity Tech model | Deep organizing at scale |

### Community Infrastructure Needed

- Discord/Slack immediately after signup (network effect)
- Newsletter with regular cadence (Buttondown or Substack)
- Local chapter toolkit (350.org model)
- Progress bars tied to collective milestones, not individual competition
- "X builders have joined" social proof on site

## 5. Economic Dashboard Technical Architecture

### Public APIs for MIND Dimensions

| Dimension | Data Sources | Refresh Rate | Coverage |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Material** | World Bank (infrastructure), FRED (housing/energy), UN Habitat | Annual-Quarterly | 190+ countries |
| **Intelligence** | OECD R&D stats, OECD AI Investment, UNESCO education data | Annual | 40-190 countries |
| **Network** | Meta Social Capital Atlas, World Values Survey, ITU connectivity | Varies (annual) | 70-190 countries |
| **Diversity** | Cultural: UNESCO, Economic: World Bank Gini, Ecological: IUCN | Annual | Variable |

### Key Technical Findings

- **DBnomics** aggregates 80+ providers (700M+ time series) — single entry point for most MIND data
- **World Bank API** is the most mature: free, well-documented, high coverage
- **OECD Data Explorer** covers developed nations with higher granularity
- **Meta's Social Capital Atlas** is the best available data for Network dimension (US only currently)
- **Biggest challenge: standardization across sources**, not data availability

### Recommended Architecture

```
Data Layer:     DBnomics + World Bank API + OECD + custom scrapers
Cache Layer:    Edge CDN with 24hr TTL (economic data doesn't change hourly)
Compute Layer:  Serverless functions for MIND score calculation
Visual Layer:   D3.js or Observable Plot for interactive dashboards
Hosting:        Vercel/Cloudflare Pages (static + edge functions)
```

### Existing Dashboard Precedents

- **OECD Better Life Index:** User-weighted, interactive, 11 dimensions — closest UX model
- **Gapminder:** Animation over time, country comparison — proven engagement pattern
- **Our World in Data:** Open-source, chart embedding, academic credibility
- **Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC):** Gold standard for trade flow visualization

## 6. Movement Website Best Practices

### What the Best Sites Do

1. **Lead with one iconic visual** — DEAL's Doughnut, OEC's trade flows. For MIND: the M×I×N×D equation or Zone Zero simulator
2. **Progressive disclosure** — simple overview first, drill-down on click. Don't teach the full framework on page 1
3. **Localize data** — city/country-specific numbers make abstract metrics personal
4. **Segment navigation by audience** — student, policymaker, practitioner, funder need different entry points
5. **Tools before theory** — playable demos convert better than explanatory text

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Jargon at the front door (insider vocabulary repels newcomers)
- Explaining everything on the homepage (earn trust before teaching)
- Theory without tools (every concept page needs a "start here")
- No onramp for different audiences
- Complexity theater (rich dashboards that require technical literacy)

### Technical Performance

- Split monolithic HTML into modules (5127 lines is unworkable)
- Cap particle count on mobile (4000 + 3200 particles will lag)
- Use `prefers-reduced-motion` guards (WCAG 2.3.3 compliance)
- Defer Three.js and GSAP loading
- Add `font-display: swap` for web fonts
